Dynasty Trust Parameters

Typically funded with discounted non-voting shares (LLC/C-Corp/family entity) so the economic value transferred exceeds the reported gift value. Grantor typically retains no income rights; beneficiaries (children/grandchildren) receive the trust. If structured as a grantor trust, the grantor pays the trust's income tax each year — an additional tax-free wealth transfer to beneficiaries (Rev. Rul. 2004-64).

QOZ Parameters

§1400Z-2 lets you defer eligible capital gains by investing the gain amount (up to the full gain) into a Qualified Opportunity Fund within 180 days of recognition. Deferral runs until the earlier of disposition or 12/31/2026. If held 10+ years, all post-investment appreciation is tax-free (basis steps to FMV on sale). Leave the amount at 0 to default to the remaining CG tax.

QSBS §1202 Exclusion Parameters

Requires C-Corp stock, originally issued to the holder. Pre-OBBBA (acquired before 7/4/2025): 5+ year hold = 100% exclusion, cap = max($10M, 10× basis), $50M gross-asset ceiling at issuance. Post-OBBBA (7/4/2025+): tiered — 3-yr = 50%, 4-yr = 75%, 5+ yr = 100%; cap = max($15M, 10× basis); $75M gross-asset ceiling. State treatment varies — CA, PA, MS, AL, NJ do not conform.

⚠️ §1202 Eligibility Attestations
Every test below must be satisfied. Any "No" disqualifies the entire §1202 exclusion — the tool zeroes the saving until all four are confirmed.

§1031 Like-Kind Exchange Parameters

Investment real estate only (no primary residences). Replacement property must be identified within 45 days and acquired within 180 days of sale. Cash "boot" taken out is taxable. This defers — not eliminates — the gain.

§199A Qualified Business Income Deduction Parameters

20% deduction on QBI from pass-through entities (S-Corps, partnerships, LLCs, sole prop). Phase-outs apply above the taxable income threshold (>$241,950 single / >$483,900 joint for 2024). Specified Service Trades or Businesses (SSTB) lose the deduction entirely above the upper threshold.

SLAT (Spousal Lifetime Access Trust) Parameters

Grantor funds an irrevocable trust for the benefit of their spouse; transfer uses lifetime gift/estate exemption. Spouse can request distributions, giving the grantor indirect access while the assets and future growth escape the estate. Reciprocal Trust Doctrine: if both spouses create SLATs for each other, terms must differ materially or IRS may collapse both back into the estate.

IDGT (Intentionally Defective Grantor Trust) Parameters

Grantor sells appreciating assets to the IDGT in exchange for an installment note at the AFR. The trust is a grantor trust for income tax (grantor pays tax on trust income, further reducing the estate) but not for estate tax (trust assets outside the estate). Appreciation above the AFR transfers to beneficiaries gift-tax-free.

Must equal or exceed the §1274(d) AFR for the month of sale. For 9-yr term use mid-term AFR. Note rate below AFR triggers §7872 gift-of-interest recharacterization.

QPRT (Qualified Personal Residence Trust) Parameters

Grantor transfers a primary or one additional residence into an irrevocable trust, retaining the right to live there for a fixed term. At the end of the term, the residence passes to beneficiaries (typically children). The taxable gift is discounted because the grantor's retained use has value.

Mortality risk: if the grantor dies during the term, the residence is pulled back into the estate and the strategy fails. Term should be set aggressively (short) for older grantors. After term, grantor must pay fair-market rent to the trust to continue occupancy — that rent further shifts wealth to beneficiaries.

CRUT (Charitable Remainder Unitrust) Parameters

Like a CRAT, but payouts are a fixed percentage of the trust's current value (re-valued annually) instead of a fixed dollar amount. Payouts grow with trust value — built-in inflation hedge. Minimum 5% unitrust rate, 10% minimum present-value deduction required.

CLT (Charitable Lead Trust) Parameters

Inverse of CRT — charity receives income stream for a fixed term, then remainder passes to heirs. Best when expected growth materially exceeds §7520 rate. Zero-out structures (remainder valued near $0 for gift tax, then outperforming assets transfer tax-free).
